Stan Lee

The Mastermind Behind Marvel Comics

A Timeline of Stan Lee's Life:

  • 1922 - Born in New York City, New York
  • 1939 - Hired as an office assistant at Timely Comics, the company that would eventually become Marvel Comics
  • 1941 - Writes his first comic book text filler, under the pseudonym "Stan Lee," which eventually becomes his legal name
  • 1942 - Enlists in the U.S. Army, serving in the Signal Corps and later in the Training Film Division during World War II
  • 1947 - Marries Joan Clayton Boocock, who remains his wife for nearly 70 years until her death in 2017
  • 1961 - Co-creates the Fantastic Four with Jack Kirby, revitalizing the superhero genre and starting the Marvel Age of comics
  • 1962 - Co-creates the Fantastic Four with Jack Kirby, revitalizing the superhero genre and starting the Marvel Age of comics
  • 1963 - Launches the X-Men and the Avengers, expanding the Marvel Universe with a team of unique and diverse superheroes
  • 1972 - Becomes publisher and editorial director of Marvel Comics, stepping away from regular writing duties
  • 1980 - Moves to California to develop Marvel's TV and movie properties, paving the way for the future of superhero films
  • 2000s - Makes cameo appearances in nearly every Marvel film, delighting fans with his humorous and unexpected on-screen presence
  • 2010 - Forms POW! Entertainment, continuing to create and develop new characters and stories
  • 2018 - Passes away at the age of 95, leaving behind a legacy that transformed popular culture

“No one has had more of an impact on my career and everything we do at Marvel Studios than Stan Lee, Stan leaves an extraordinary legacy that will outlive us all. Our thoughts are with his daughter, his family, and the millions of fans who have been forever touched by Stan’s genius, charisma, and heart. Excelsior!"

--Kevin Feige
